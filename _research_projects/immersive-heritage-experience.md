This project extends immersive research toward cultural and educational access. Instead of assuming premium equipment, it asks how low-cost virtual reality can still deliver meaningful heritage experiences.

The focus is on practical deployment: simpler pipelines, broader accessibility, and clearer interpretation of spatial heritage environments. That makes the work relevant not only to preservation research, but also to institutions that need feasible public-facing immersive experiences.
